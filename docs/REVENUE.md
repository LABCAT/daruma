# Revenue — Project Daruma

## Models by Brand

### Daruma Toolbox
- **Model:** Monthly subscription
- **Price range:** $5–$20/mo per tool
- **Target:** 15–50 customers per app to hit $500/mo
- **Churn focus:** Keep tools genuinely useful so people don't cancel

### Daruma Dojo
- **Model:** Free + ads + optional IAP
- **Ad revenue:** Requires volume — ~50k–100k MAU per app for meaningful revenue
- **IAP options:** Remove ads ($0.99–$2.99 one-time), cosmetics, level packs
- **Strategy:** Many small apps compound — 20–30 games each with modest traffic adds up

## ASO Strategy (Daruma Dojo)

App Store Optimisation is the primary discovery channel for Dojo — no paid ads needed if ASO is done well.

### Process
1. Research keywords **before** naming or building the app
2. Find keywords with decent search volume and weak competition (old, ugly, or poorly rated top apps)
3. Build title and subtitle around the primary keyword
4. Launch, gather initial ratings, watch ranking
5. Iterate keywords based on what is actually driving installs

### Tools
- **AppFollow / AppFigures** — keyword volume and difficulty (free tiers sufficient to start)
- **AppMagic** — revenue estimates for existing apps (sanity check)
- **Manual App Store search** — autocomplete reveals real search behaviour

### What to look for
- Low competition keyword with decent volume
- Top apps rated 3.5 stars or lower
- Apps that look dated or bloated
- No beautiful, focused alternative exists yet

## SEO Strategy (Daruma Toolbox)

- Niche utility tools rank on Google for specific long-tail queries
- Target: "invoice generator for NZ sole traders", "shift roster calculator", etc.
- Content is minimal — the tool itself is the SEO asset
- Supplemented by Reddit niche communities and direct outreach
